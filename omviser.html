omviser

omviser’s platform aims to empower International Students by providing location-agnostic support networks for & during their college admission journey in the United States.

ux designer3 month internshipdesign team

context

omviser is a platform that connects international students with peer advisers and educational advisers to guide them through college (undergraduate and graduate) admissions processes in the United States. It is a system that gives the students the best opportunity to access higher education through the pairing of an adviser with experience studying in the U.S and their educational process.
The platform hopes to empower international students by providing location-agnostic support networks for and during their college admission and application process.

main goals

  • design a responsive site that makes mentorship more accessible
  • provide a platform to coach international students
  • make advising accessible regardless of socioeconomic conditions or geographic boundaries

site mapping

To begin the ideation process, we developed a site map in order to organize the specifics of our full interface and allocate team responsibilities accordingly.

By targeting clicks directed from the homepage body, we created flows for mentees based on primary tasks: geting started, connecting with advisers, and finding podcasts and blogs.

design

low fidelity

I started sketching out paper wireframes to include basic content and static visuals to get an understanding of the overall website

high fidelity

Through gauging user and team feedback from my low-fidelity designs, I moved towards designing high-fidelity prototypes. By exploring multiple iterations through high-fidelity mockups and employing rapid iterative usability testing measures, my designs were validated directly by user data.

conclusion

Although my time at omviser is done, the UI/UX team plans to move forth with this final design iteration and perform usability testing to evaluate user feedback and enhance the platform. Usability testing would include a task-focused plan and would be evaluated using the following metrics to address both usability success and overall product success:
  1. Task Success Rate
  2. Time Taken Per Task
  3. Net Promoter Score
  4. Customer Satisfaction Scale
Reflecting, this has been a very rich experience, and I've learned a lot about how different forms of research find their way into every stage of the UX process. Thanks for reading!
