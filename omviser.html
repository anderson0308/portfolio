omviser

Your College Advisor

Overview
Omviser is digital platform that provides guidance and advising to international students applying to undergraduate and graduate programs.
Role
UX/UI Designer
Timeline
June 2020 - September 2020
Team
Design Team

Context

omviser is a platform that connects international students with peer advisers and educational advisers to guide them through college (undergraduate and graduate) admissions processes in the United States. It is a system that gives the students the best opportunity to access higher education through the pairing of an adviser with experience studying in the U.S and their educational process.
The platform hopes to empower international students by providing location-agnostic support networks for and during their college admission and application process. The main goal of this project is to design a responsive site that makes mentorship more accessible, irrespective of socioeconomic conditions or geographic boundaries, and provide a platform to coach international students.

Problem Statement

How might we mitigate the struggles of international students during their college application & transition process with an efficient mentorship digital platorm.

Empathize

Competitive Analysis

To initiate our research process, we began by conducting a competitive analysis of three primary competitors who target high school students through educational consultancy.

Surveys

User Research

By conducting numerous interviews with a variety, we developed insights to target three different types of characters for the omviser platform:

Personas

To really get a feel for the target users, I created personas based on our research to understand their needs, wants, and frustrations.

Customer Journey Map

After creating the personas, I then mapper out the individual journey experience across the various touch points and several scenarios on the product. I made a note of their interactions, identified pages, and pain points in the said scenarios.

Ideate

Site Mapping

To begin the ideation process, we developed a site map in order to organize the specifics of our full interface and allocate team responsibilities accordingly.

User Flow Diagram

By targeting clicks directed from the homepage body, we created flows for mentees based on primary tasks: getting started, connecting with advisers, and finding podcasts and blogs.

Low-Fidelity Wireframes

To finalize the ideation phase of the project, I began the prototyping process by sketching various concepts for both the home page, blog page, and student step page, email confirmation, and filter pop-up.

Design

Final Screens

Through gauging users and team feedback from my low-fidelity designs, I moved towards designing high-fidelity prototypes. By exploring multiple iterations through high-fidelity mockups and employing rapid iterative usability testing measures, my designs were validated directly by user data.

To maintain consistency throughout the entire platform, we established a design system that would guide us with the visual treatment for the interfaces.

Conclusion

Although my time at omviser is done, the UI/UX team plans to move forth with this final design iteration and perform usability testing to evaluate user feedback and enhance the platform. Usability testing would include a task-focused plan and would be evaluated using the following metrics to address both usability success and overall product success:
  1. Task Success Rate
  2. Time Taken Per Task
  3. Net Promoter Score
  4. Customer Satisfaction Scale
Reflecting, this has been a very rich experience, and I've learned a lot about how different forms of research find their way into every stage of the UX process. Thanks for reading!
